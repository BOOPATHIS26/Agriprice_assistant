2. **Drip Irrigation**: Also known as trickle irrigation, this method involves delivering water directly to the roots of the plants through a network of tubes and drippers. It's highly efficient and water-saving, making it ideal for crops like fruits, vegetables, and flowers.
3. **Sprinkler Irrigation**: This method uses sprinklers to distribute water over a wide area, similar to rainfall. It's commonly used for crops like lawns, gardens, and orchards.
4. **Center Pivot Irrigation**: This is a type of sprinkler irrigation where a long, wheeled pipe rotates around a central pivot, distributing water in a circular pattern. It's commonly used for large-scale farming operations.
5. **Micro-Irrigation**: This is a type of irrigation that delivers water in small quantities, often through a network of tiny tubes and emitters. It's highly efficient and water-saving, making it ideal for crops like vegetables, fruits, and nuts.
6. **Flood Irrigation**: This method involves flooding the entire field with water, which is then absorbed by the soil. It's commonly used for crops like paddy and sugarcane.
7. **Basin Irrigation**: This method involves surrounding a crop with a shallow ditch or basin, which is then filled with water. It's commonly used for crops like fruits and vegetables.
8. **Subsurface Irrigation**: This method involves burying pipes or tubes underground, which deliver water directly to the roots of the plants. It's highly efficient and water-saving, making it ideal for crops like corn and soybeans.
